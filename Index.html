Mini Painting

Painting Process

  1. Cutting & Assembly
    1. Hobby Clippers for Snipping
    2. Avoid Clipping to much at once for simplicity in assembly
    3. Clip near parts but not flush
    4. Cleanup with hobby knife (slice nubs, avoid cutting model, scrape backwards along mold lines for removal)
    5. Snip push-fit pins and glue model together.
    6. Superglue or if model is polystyrene use plastic glue
    7. Clamp for glue setting
    8. âš ï¸ Don't Glue to bases until both are painted!
  2. Cleaning
    1. Minis need to be cleaned in soapy water prior to priming (TODO)
  3. Priming
    1. Place Models on Spraying Surface(Cardboard or whatever) using tape/putty
    2. Shake then Spraypaint or Brush On Primer (Ensure low humidity and warm primer)
    3. 2-3 light passes in black. 1 coat every 20 minutes to dry. (Ensure Dry Environment)
    4. 1 pass in white from ariel perspective (zenithal priming)
    5. âš ï¸ Avoid Pooling Primer on model!
  4. Handle Setup
    1. Attach mini's to handle either using putty,magnets,or tape.
    2. OR Buy RGG360
  5. Prepare Wetpallete & Brushes
  6. Painting
    1. Basecoat Mini (often two coats)
      1. Skin (or lowest layer equivalent)
      2. Armor
      3. Weapons (or other metallic paint drybrushing)
      4. Accessories
    2. Painting Bases
  7. Glue Model to Base
    1. Plastic Glue if Base and Mini are plastic (rub away paint at connecting points)
    2. Super Glue otherwise.
    3. Green stuff applied to feet helps glue stick to uneven/rough base.
  8. Review
    1. Error Correction
    2. Color Changing
  9. Detailing
    1. Washing
      1. Wash Base
      2. Wash in same order as Basecoat
      3. âš ï¸ Avoid Pooling Washes on model!
    2. Highlights (drybrush)
      1. Consider highlights for every color on Mini
      2. Silver on metal
      3. Tiny details (glow bits in eyes, jewelry, etc)
    3. Eyes
      1. Darken Wash in sockets
      2. White Oval
      3. Black Pupil
      4. Clean up with skin tone
  10. Varnishing
    1. Gloss varnish for protection.
    2. Matte varnish to dull gloss.

General Tips

Contrast Paints = Gamesworkshop
Speed paints = armypainter
Using plastic glue instead of mini's built in assembly spokes is more flush.
Zenathil Priming (spraying top with white)
Guys like to use vallejo for this
Can either do base primer black and white sprayed from arial or black base, gray 40-60 degrees spray, and white aerial.

its okay to mix colors
painting flesh first is useful because its often the lowest layer and its okay to go outside of the lines at first.

  • black is a good undercoat for metallic steel or silver

    • followed up by drybrushing the metallic.

  • Brown is a good basecoat for gold.

  • Useful to have two shades of brown (ie distinguishing belt and pouch)

  • Color Transparency Varies

    • Blue often needs only one coat
    • Yellow is often very translucent

  • Darker bases good to not distract from mini

  • Basecoating is the process of laying first layer (maybe in two coats) of color and drybrushing.

  • Washes are thin paints designed to run into crevices.

    • Brown is a good washcolor for gold.
    • Black is a good wash for steel/metallic.

  • Goobertown likes citadel shade for washcolors on defined models and armor color for less defined (Citaldel for BruceLee Army Color for Vin Disel)

  • Lighter wash tones or lighter colors

  • Mixing white with base color is good for highlighting that color.

  • Mixing yellow with green/red/orange is good for highlighting

  • Silver drybrush on metal for final highlighting

  • For eyes, be steady prior, tiny brush with sharp tip, bright lights

  • Gloss varnish are great protection but the sheen is often too much and can be tamed with a follow up of matte varnish.

    • Little Deven Idea : Gloss varnish on shiny materials and maybe cover them with masking when doing the matte varnish on the rest of the figure.
    • its also fine to paint over varnish in the future.

  • You can hide seam lines when connecting 3d printer materials using the printer resin and curing with uv, then finishing with qtip n alchohol.

  • can use cooking parchment paper for wetpallete

  • CRI (color rating index), closer to 100 = more like real sunlight kinda antiquated

Tools

  • Hobby Clippers
  • Hobby Knife (dull for moldlines sharp is also usefull)
  • Super Glue or Plastic Glue/Cement (Timia Extrathin but brand doesnt really matter)
  • Primer (rustoleum painter's touch 2x primer flat black AND flat white) or fred meyer equivalent brand doesnt matter. or Airbrush primer and paint on.
  • Clamps
  • Stir Sticks (for lining up minis for priming)
  • double sided tape 3m foam
  • Putty
    • for holders(museum putty* more to learn) probably only with fancy holder
    • for attaching magnets 2part epoxy
  • wet pallet
  • Magnets
    • 25mm base: 6mm x 1.5mm
    • 32mm base: 6mm x 4mm x2mm
    • Bigger base: 10mx 2mm
    • Can put spacer between magnet and base using 2part epoxy putty (5min loktite) to get magnet closer to surface its on.
    • âš™ï¸ Ensure direction of poles is standardized
    • âš ï¸ Superglue is too weak!
  • handles (blocks of wood, poster putty or doublesided tape, bottles, magnets on mini bottoms w/ strikeplates on wooden dowel S tier)
  • Acrylic paints
    • Game color introduction box by vallejo from Goobertown video.
      • Been around a long time,dropper bottles, large product line
  • Multipack craft brushes
  • Gamesworkshop calls them washes and armypainter calls them tones
  • Baking dirt at low temp for mini dirt.
  • tea leaves from tea packet for leaves/wood debris
  • sanding sticks for mold lines
  • Plastic Cement for filling
  • Brush Cleaner
  • Cutting mat to protect table
  • Lighting (blub should be neutral/cool 5000-5500 kelvin)
    • 3400 lumens is way too much brightness
  • squeezy water bottle (tattoo bottle)
  • scrapbooking tubs for paint storage 4inch height for fitting bottles.
  • ReallyUsefulBoxesTM for model storage w/a magnet sheet on bottom
    • investigate strong magnet sheets or diy
    • also investigate non aluminum cake pans
  • monument hobbies agitators to make stuff shakeable
  • files for big burrs and sanding largescale
  • file brush for cleaning them
  • magic mix increases paint working time
